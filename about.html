About.

I'm a robotics engineer most at home in the gap between "it works in simulation" and "it works at 2 AM in a hotel bathroom." My career has been shaped by the distance between controlled demos and robust real-world deployment — and I've spent years closing it.

At Peanut Robotics, I owned the arm software stack for a commercially deployed mobile manipulator — trajectory generation, computer vision, depth camera integration, and operator tooling that cut training time from hours to minutes. At Google, I build automated biometric security testing systems for Pixel devices, designing attack trajectory libraries and leading a full ROS1→ROS2 migration.

I'm now deeply focused on the intersection of classical robotics and learned policies — behavioral cloning, diffusion policy, foundation models — and how to combine them with classical control for reliable, contact-rich manipulation. I believe the next wave of robotics will require engineers who can fluently bridge both worlds.
