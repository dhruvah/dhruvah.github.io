About.

I'm a robotics engineer interested in the intersection of technology and real-world deployment — making robots that work outside the lab, in messy environments, on useful applications.

I have four years of industry experience. Two years at Google (via AutoRoboto), where I built robotic systems for biometric security testing on Pixel devices — including a full ROS1→ROS2 migration and automated attack trajectory libraries. Two years at Peanut Robotics, where I worked on the arm software stack on a mobile manipulator built for hotel bathroom cleaning — trajectory generation, computer vision, depth camera integration, and operator tooling.

Background

Carnegie Mellon University · M.S. Mechanical Engineering, Robotics Research (2020–2022, 4.00 GPA). NASA ULI-sponsored research on robotic post-processing of additive manufacturing parts at CERLAB.

Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology · B.S. Mechanical Engineering, Magna Cum Laude (2016–2020, 3.71 GPA). Represented Rose-Hulman and the USA at the E²Festa Global Capstone Design Competition in South Korea.

Currently exploring

I'm now focused on the intersection of classical robotics and learned policies — behavioral cloning, diffusion policy, foundation models — and how to combine them with classical control for reliable, contact-rich manipulation tasks. I've been building hands-on with the XLErobot to work through these ideas in practice.
