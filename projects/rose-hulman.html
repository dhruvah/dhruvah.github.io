Year-long capstone · 4-person team

Automated eating apparatus for a quadriplegic patient

Our client, Gary Wallace, was quadriplegic and needed help with every meal, three times a day. Commercial robotic feeding arms ran $5,000–$10,000 — out of reach. Over three iterative prototypes across the year, our four-person team built a low-cost alternative: an Arduino MEGA driving stepper motors and (later) a linear actuator, with a magnetic, swappable spoon head, a joystick that mounts to the client's chair, limit switches for positioning, and adjustable legs for different table heights.

The first prototype took us to the E²Festa global capstone competition in South Korea, representing Rose-Hulman and the USA and placing 10th, recognized for innovative, cost-effective design. The final working prototype came in at roughly $150 — a fraction of the commercial options — with full documentation: parts lists, engineering drawings, assembly instructions, and code. Gary passed away before the final prototype was finished; the team's intent was to keep developing the design for broader use by people living with paralysis.

Controls · MATLAB

Inverse kinematics via a distal-teacher network

Learned the inverse kinematics of a 3-DOF planar manipulator. First trained a network on the forward kinematics from 100 random joint configurations, then added an inverse network and trained both together with the distal-teacher method and Levenberg-Marquardt optimization. Given a desired end-effector position, the controller network returns a valid set of joint angles — built from scratch in MATLAB without any neural-network libraries.

Controls · Simulink

Fuzzy-logic controllers for autonomous parking

Mamdani fuzzy controllers for autonomous back-up and parallel parking. The back-parking controller used three input membership functions and 27 rules and stayed robust across hard cases like double-diamond starts; the parallel-parking controller used four inputs and 43 rules, adding front-axle displacement. Built with the MATLAB Fuzzy Toolbox and Simulink.
