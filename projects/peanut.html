Context

Peanut Robotics builds a mobile manipulator that cleans hotel bathrooms — a deceptively hard task. Each bathroom has different geometry, fixtures, and floor heights. The arm needs to track contact-rich cleaning trajectories, swap tools mid-task, and recover gracefully when something doesn't go to plan. I was the engineer responsible for making all of that work, day in and day out, in real hotels.

Approach

[TODO — explain the technical approach.] A high-level outline:

  • Trajectory generation: TOPPRA for time-optimal parameterization, integrated with ros_control for synchronized tool actuation.
  • Vision pipeline: ICP + RANSAC point cloud registration to handle geometric variation across bathrooms.
  • Depth camera selection: Evaluated 6 systems against accuracy, range, noise, and ROS support; selected Orbbec Gemini2 and engineered a C++ bringup layer with auto-recovery for 4 simultaneous cameras.
  • Calibration validation: Deployed Optitrack motion tracking to quantify arm droop profiles and verify calibration accuracy under load.
  • Operator tooling: Designed and shipped a live trajectory recording system — the largest single win.
